Projects

Project 1

The Meditations begin with very personal notes, but even these notes which mentions important teachers, mentors and family members, mean something to the reader as well. For example, the first reads “From my grandfather Verus I learned good morals and the government of my temper.” From this straightforward sentiment, we can understand that Aurelius is interested in education, virtue, prudence, reason, and temperance.

Project 2

Marcus Aurelius taught that true tranquility arises from mastering one’s inner self, not from external conditions or material possessions. He urged us to recognize that while external events are often beyond our control, our reactions and attitudes remain fully within our power. In today’s world, we frequently chase external validation—social media likes, career promotions, or material acquisitions—to feel fulfilled or happy. Yet, Aurelius reminds us that lasting peace comes from within. Practices such as meditation, journaling, or simply reflecting quietly each day can help us nurture internal calm and resilience, enabling us to face life’s turbulence with grace.
